When physiotherapy?

A physiotherapist treats complaints of muscles, tendons, joints and the nervous system. The goal is for you to move safely and with confidence again in work, sport and daily life.

Common complaints

  • Sports injuries and overuse
  • Back, neck, shoulder and knee pain
  • Posture-related and work-related complaints
  • Recovery after surgery or immobilisation (e.g. cast or brace)
  • Gait and stability problems
  • Muscle and tendon complaints related to strength training or work

Possible treatment options

  • Sports massage & deep tissue massage
  • Joint and spinal mobilisations
  • Dry needling
  • Fascia treatment
  • TENS/EMS (electrotherapy where appropriate)
  • Rehabilitation training after injury or surgery
  • Functional training (strength, stability, coordination, mobility, endurance)
  • Posture and movement analysis
  • Taping (kinesio tape / sports tape)
  • Trigger point therapy
  • Lifestyle and exercise advice
  • Breathing and relaxation techniques

Is your question or complaint not listed? Feel free to get in touch so we can determine together whether physiotherapy is appropriate or whether referral is needed.

Collaboration

Where needed, coordination with your GP, specialist or the dietitian within Phrona.

Our approach

  1. Intake & assessment (45–60 min): conversation about your complaints, tests of strength, mobility and coordination, and explanation of the cause.
  2. Treatment plan: combination of hands-on techniques and an exercise programme, including agreements on frequency and duration.
  3. Follow-up (30 min): monitoring progress, progressing or adjusting exercises and guiding return to sport/work.

What to bring

  • Comfortable (sports) clothing that allows you to move freely
  • Referral from GP or specialist if available (not always required)
  • Overview of medication and relevant medical information
  • Imaging reports if available (e.g. MRI/ultrasound reports)
  • Insurance details

Fees & reimbursement

Physiotherapy is often (partly) reimbursed from supplementary insurance. For some indications, reimbursement from basic insurance may apply. We are happy to think along with you and can review your policy together.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a referral?

In most cases, no. With Direct Access Physiotherapy (DTF) you can make an appointment directly. In case of doubt or complex complaints, we consult with your GP.

How many sessions will I need?

This depends on the complaint and your goal. On average 3–8 sessions, with home exercises as an important part of recovery.

What should I wear?

Sports or comfortable clothing that allows you to move freely. You can bring a towel if you like.
