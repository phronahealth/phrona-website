About me

I work as a dietitian in care institutions with a broad target group: from people who come in for rehabilitation to older adults moving into long-term care. I provide nutritional advice to optimise nutritional status and support recovery as effectively as possible. I also have extensive experience with people who can barely eat or no longer eat at all, where medical nutrition or tube feeding is required. In primary care I have guided many clients with overweight and obesity.

In my younger years I struggled with being overweight myself and was treated by several dietitians. Real change came when my mother signed me up for a gym. There I discovered my passion for fitness and powerlifting, which I practised for several years at amateur level. Nowadays I enjoy combat sports such as wrestling and kickboxing.

I see it as a major pitfall that health is often approached in a very black-and-white way. Health is multifactorial: nutrition, movement, culture, home situation and mental load all play a role. I place a lot of value on the “why” question. Why does someone overeat? Why does someone barely eat at all? Once this becomes clear, a much more effective treatment plan can be created.

Together with my brother I want to clarify the “grey area” around movement, nutrition and health. This led to the creation of Phrona, where we take the time to share our insights and knowledge on these concepts with our clients. On this page I give an impression of what my guidance looks like.

Approach

Guidance follows clear steps, with room to adjust along the way.

Intake

Your treatment goals, medical history, lifestyle and expectations are discussed.

Analysis

Insight into eating pattern, physical activity, work and home situation to identify patterns and opportunities.

Personalised plan

Clear goals and practical agreements on meals, portions and lifestyle.

Coaching

Regular consultations to discuss progress and address obstacles together.

Embedding

Working on habits and environment so that changes are sustainable in the long term.

Evaluation

Reviewing results, adjusting where needed and agreeing on follow-up or completion.

“Small, consistent steps work better than strict, short periods. My role is to make it doable and clear for you.”
— Serhat Erdikici

Frequently asked questions

How often are the consultations?

On average every 2–4 weeks, depending on your goals and schedule.

Do I have to count calories?

Not necessarily. We choose a method that suits you, such as portion sizes, planning or using an app.

Do you work together with others?

Yes, where needed I coordinate with the physiotherapist or with your GP.
