When dietetics?

A dietitian is a paramedical professional specialised in nutrition and behaviour. The dietitian can support you with nutrition for medical conditions, achieving your health goals or improving sports performance.

Health & performance

  • Healthier living
  • Lifestyle coaching: energy, sleep, stress eating
  • Overweight and obesity
  • Underweight and malnutrition
  • Optimising sports performance
  • Metabolic syndrome

Medical conditions

  • Stomach and intestinal complaints, IBS
  • Preparation for and aftercare following bariatric surgery
  • High cholesterol and high blood pressure
  • COPD (emphysema)
  • Kidney and liver diseases
  • Oncology and recovery during/after illness
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Gout
  • Coeliac disease
  • Dietary support for (burn) wounds

Is your question or condition not listed? Feel free to send a message and we will look together at how the dietitian can support you.

Measuring & evaluating

Progress is monitored with weight, waist circumference and targeted questionnaires.

Practical approach

Concrete advice on meals, planning and daily structure that fits your lifestyle.

Culturally sensitive

Consideration for habits, family and social context.

Our approach

  1. Intake (60 min): goals, medical information, nutritional analysis.
  2. Plan & advice (30 min): practical agreements and dietary guidelines (e.g., sample menus).
  3. Follow-up (30 min): adjusting based on progress and challenges.

What to bring

  • Current medication/allergies and relevant medical information
  • Three-day food diary (optional)
  • Insurance details

Fees & reimbursement

Dietetics is generally covered for 3 hours per year under basic insurance; additional hours may be reimbursed via supplementary insurance, depending on your policy. Claims are processed according to the contract with your insurer.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a referral?

Not required. Reimbursement depends on your policy. We can help you check.

Can the programme be done online?

Yes, intake and follow-ups can be conducted online when appropriate.

How long does a programme last?

On average 3–6 consultations over 2–3 months, depending on your goal.
