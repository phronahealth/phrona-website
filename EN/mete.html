About me

I work as a physiotherapist in primary care, helping people with a wide range of complaints: from long-term pain and chronic conditions to sports and shoulder complaints. My passion for the profession partly stems from my own experience with chronic issues. For years I did not feel heard and received conflicting advice. Working on your health is not as black-and-white as it sometimes seems. Through trial and error I developed a more effective way of working. By coincidence my brother is a dietitian, and we were able to use each other’s insights in this process.

Together with my brother, I want to shed light on the “grey area” around movement, nutrition and health. This led to the creation of Phrona, where we take the time to share our insights and knowledge on these concepts with our clients. On this page I outline how my treatment process typically looks.

Approach

Guidance follows clear steps, with attention to both your complaints and your load capacity in daily life and sport. The text in each step flows within the same section.

Intake

Extensive conversation about your complaints, load moments, sport, work and goals.

Assessment

Functional assessment of joints, muscles and posture to determine the cause of your symptoms.

Treatment plan

Clear explanation, expectations and a plan with exercises, therapy and graded loading.

Exercise therapy

Targeted exercises to improve strength, stability and mobility.

Return to activity

Stepwise programmes for work, sport and daily activities, tailored to your goals.

Evaluation

Reviewing results, adjusting where needed and agreeing on follow-up or discharge.

“Recovery is more than reducing pain. We work towards what you want to be able to do again: moving with confidence in work, sport and daily life.”
— Mete Erdikici

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a referral?

In many cases you can come directly without a referral. If in doubt, you can contact your GP or the practice.

How often are the sessions?

On average once a week during the build-up phase, then less often depending on your recovery.

Can I continue training during treatment?

Often yes. Together we adjust load and exercises so that you can stay as active as possible in a safe way.
