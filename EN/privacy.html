2. Scope and definitions

This privacy policy applies to all processing of personal data of (potential) clients and website visitors of Phrona.

Personal data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. Health data includes data that provide information about a person’s physical or mental health. These data are special categories of personal data and receive extra protection under the law.

3. What personal data Phrona processes

3.1 General personal data

– Name, address, place of residence
– Date of birth
– Telephone number
– Email address
– Health insurer and policy number
– Citizen Service Number (BSN), where necessary for declarations

3.2 Health data

– Information about your health, complaints and treatment request
– Medical history insofar as relevant to the dietetic treatment
– Measurement data (for example weight, height, blood values if shared)
– Treatment plan, recommendations and progress notes

3.3 Data via the contact form

When you use the contact form on the website, Phrona processes the data you provide, such as your name, email address, telephone number and the content of your message. These data are used to answer your question or to contact you.

3.4 Website and usage data

The website may process technical data such as IP address, browser data and time of visit. Some of this information is commonly recorded by the hosting provider in log files, for example to keep the website secure and stable.

Phrona may use cookies and similar technologies. The specific cookie use (for example functional, analytical and/or tracking cookies) will be described in a separate cookie overview: [cookie policy/cookie overview to be added later].

4. Purposes of processing

Phrona processes your personal data for the following purposes:

– Providing dietetic care (assessment, advice, treatment and guidance)
– Maintaining an (electronic) medical file
– Maintaining contact, for example to schedule or change appointments
– Submitting claims for provided care to health insurers or sending invoices to you
– Complying with legal obligations, such as record-keeping and tax retention duties
– Answering questions submitted via the contact form, email or WhatsApp
– Improving and securing the website (technical and statistical purposes, where applicable)

5. Legal basis for processing

Phrona only processes personal data if there is a valid legal basis under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

– Performance of the treatment agreement: to provide dietetic care
– Legal obligation: such as the obligation to keep a medical record and to comply with statutory tax retention periods
– Consent: for example, for sharing information with your GP, physiotherapist or other healthcare providers, and for certain forms of communication
– Legitimate interest: for limited technical and statistical analyses of website use and to ensure system security, provided that your privacy interests do not outweigh this interest

6.2 Health insurers

For claim submissions, Phrona may share data with health insurers. This is done only insofar as necessary to declare and account for the care provided.

8. Security of personal data

Phrona takes appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against loss, unauthorised access, misuse and unlawful alteration.

Examples of such measures include:

– Use of secure connections (https/SSL) for the website
– Restricted access to client data: only for the practitioner(s) who need these data
– Use of secure systems for record-keeping and communication with other healthcare providers (such as Zorgmail)
– Strong passwords and, where possible, additional security layers
– No transmission of extensive medical data via unsecured channels such as standard email or WhatsApp

9. Rights of data subjects

As a data subject you have several rights in relation to your personal data:

– Right of access to your record and personal data
– Right to rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data
– Right to erasure of data (where this is not in conflict with statutory retention obligations)
– Right to restriction of processing in certain cases
– Right to data portability
– Right to withdraw consent previously given

You can exercise these rights by contacting Phrona using the contact details provided in this policy. Phrona may ask you to identify yourself to ensure that the request is genuinely made by you.

11. Minors

For clients under 16 years of age, dietetic care is only provided with the involvement and consent of the parent(s) or legal guardian(s), insofar as required by law. Personal and health data of minors are handled with particular care.

12. Questions or complaints

If you have questions about this privacy policy or about the way Phrona handles your personal data, you can contact us using the contact details listed in section 1.

If you are not satisfied with the way your question or complaint has been handled, you may submit a complaint to the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) via www.autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.
