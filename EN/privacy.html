2. Scope and definitions

This privacy policy applies to all processing of personal data of (potential) clients and website visitors of Phrona.

Personal data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. Health data includes data that provide information about a person's physical or mental health. These are special categories of personal data and receive extra protection under the law.

3. What personal data Phrona processes

3.1 General personal data

– Name, address, place of residence
– Date of birth
– Telephone number
– Email address
– Health insurer and policy number
– Citizen Service Number (BSN), where necessary for declarations

3.2 Health data

– Information about your health, complaints and treatment request
– Medical history insofar as relevant to the dietetic treatment
– Measurement data (for example weight, height, blood values if shared)
– Treatment plan, recommendations and progress notes

3.3 Data via the contact form

When you use the contact form on the website, Phrona processes the data you provide, such as your name, email address, telephone number and the content of your message. These data are used to answer your question or to contact you.

3.4 Website and usage data

The website may process technical data such as IP address, browser data and time of visit. Some of this information is commonly recorded by the hosting provider in log files to keep the website secure and stable.

4. Purposes of processing

Phrona processes personal data for the following purposes:

– Providing dietetic care (assessment, advice, treatment and guidance)
– Maintaining an electronic medical file via JAMES
– Maintaining contact, for example to schedule or change appointments
– Submitting claims for provided care to health insurers or sending invoices
– Complying with legal obligations, such as record-keeping and tax retention duties
– Answering questions submitted via the contact form, email or WhatsApp
– Improving and securing the website

5. Legal basis for processing

Phrona only processes personal data on a valid legal basis under the GDPR:

– Performance of the treatment agreement: to provide dietetic care
– Legal obligation: such as the obligation to keep a medical record and to comply with statutory tax retention periods
– Consent: for example, for sharing information with your GP, physiotherapist or other healthcare providers
– Legitimate interest: for limited technical analyses of website use and to ensure system security, provided that your privacy interests do not outweigh this interest

6. Sharing personal data with third parties

Phrona only shares data with third parties when this is necessary for the performance of treatment, for administrative purposes, or where required by law.

6.1 Other healthcare providers

Information is shared with other healthcare providers, such as your GP, specialist or physiotherapist, only after you have given explicit consent, unless a legal provision requires otherwise.

6.2 Health insurers

For claim submissions, Phrona may share data with health insurers. This is done only insofar as necessary to declare and account for the care provided.

6.3 Processors

For registering client data, scheduling appointments, sending invoices and hosting the website, Phrona uses external service providers, including:

– JAMES (electronic health record)
– GitHub Pages (website hosting)
– Siilo (secure communication with healthcare providers)

Where required, data processing agreements have been concluded with these parties.

6.4 No sale of data

Phrona never sells personal data to third parties.

7. Retention periods

Phrona does not retain personal data longer than necessary, unless a legal retention obligation requires a longer period:

– Medical file: at least 15 years from the date of the last contact, or longer if required by law
– Financial administration (invoices, etc.): 7 years, in line with tax regulations
– Data from the contact form: until your question has been handled, plus a maximum of 12 months
– Log files: in accordance with the hosting provider's log retention policy

8. Security of personal data

Phrona takes appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against loss, unauthorised access, misuse and unlawful alteration:

– Secure connections (https/SSL) for the website
– Restricted access to client data: only for the practitioner who needs these data
– Secure systems for record-keeping and communication with other healthcare providers (Siilo)
– No transmission of medical data via unsecured channels

9. Rights of data subjects

You have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

– Right of access to your record and personal data
– Right to rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data
– Right to erasure of data (where not in conflict with statutory retention obligations)
– Right to restriction of processing in certain cases
– Right to data portability
– Right to withdraw consent previously given

You can exercise these rights by contacting Phrona using the details in section 1. Phrona may ask you to identify yourself to verify that the request is genuinely made by you.

10. Minors

For clients under 16 years of age, dietetic care is only provided with the involvement and consent of the parent(s) or legal guardian(s), insofar as required by law. Personal and health data of minors are handled with particular care.

11. Questions or complaints

If you have questions about this privacy policy or about the way Phrona handles personal data, please contact us using the details listed in section 1.

If you are not satisfied with the way your question or complaint has been handled, you may submit a complaint to the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) via autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.

12. Changes to this privacy policy

Phrona may amend this privacy policy from time to time, for example if legislation changes or working methods are adjusted. The most recent version will always be available on phrona.nl.

This version applies from 1 January 2026 and replaces all previous versions.
