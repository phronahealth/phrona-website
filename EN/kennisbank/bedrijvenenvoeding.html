Companies and nutrition

Updated: November 2025

Over recent years our eating habits have become increasingly dependent on external sources outside our own kitchen. We have gradually become used to supermarkets and restaurants providing and preparing our food. This shift is partly driven by the convenience we seek ourselves. From an evolutionary point of view, our brain wants to conserve energy and prefers efforts with low intensity and a high nutritional payoff. This mechanism made sense for survival in earlier conditions, such as those of hunter-gatherers.

The food industry makes use of this natural mechanism. Products rich in fat, sugar and salt activate our reward system. The quick availability and strong appeal of foods high in fat, sugar and salt tap into the natural tendencies of our brain, leading to overconsumption and health problems.

The key lies in awareness. By understanding how companies respond to our preferences, we can make more conscious choices.
